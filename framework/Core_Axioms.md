# Core Axioms of CCHTA

1. Physiology and psychology are inseparable regulatory processes.
2. Safety perception governs biological stability.
3. Sensations are system signals, not inherently pathological.
4. Stabilization precedes interpretation and functional demand.
5. Regulation is a trainable skill.
6. Gradual adaptation produces durable change.
7. Transparency enhances physiological safety.
8. Patients are primary sources of internal system data.
9. Co-regulation may precede self-regulation.
10. Recovery is defined by stability, function, and autonomy.
